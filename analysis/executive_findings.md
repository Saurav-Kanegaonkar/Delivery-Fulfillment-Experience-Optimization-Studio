# Executive Findings

## What I Analyzed

I modeled 840 daily fulfillment rows across 10 delivery scenarios for a coffeehouse and restaurant delivery program. The analysis connects SLA, order accuracy, courier wait, store capacity, customer contacts, partner cancellations, and sentiment to a product roadmap.

## Findings

- Late-day promotion spikes are the top operating risk, with 59.6% under-25-minute SLA, 5.7 minutes of courier handoff wait, and 93.1% modeled capacity utilization.
- Capacity-aware delivery promise logic is the strongest initiative, with a modeled $41,338 monthly value and 78% confidence.
- Food bundle readiness and driver-arrival pacing are also pilot-ready because they have clear owners, measurable acceptance criteria, and direct customer experience impact.
- The work should be managed as a product-operations loop, not only as reporting. Each opportunity needs a requirement, owner, test metric, and partner cadence.

## Recommendation

Pilot capacity-aware promise logic in high-volume morning and promotion stores, while launching food bundle readiness as standard work in food-heavy cafes. Stand up a weekly partner exception review so third-party asks, fulfillment defects, and internal constraints are prioritized with the same decision rules.
