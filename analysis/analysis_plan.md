# Analysis Plan

## Objective

Identify which delivery fulfillment improvements should move into discovery, pilot, or monitoring when customer experience, store capacity, partner requests, and operational effort compete.

## Inputs

- Fulfillment segment assumptions by channel, store scenario, owner, baseline volume, capacity pressure, food attachment, prep time, handoff wait, and customer sentiment.
- Twelve weeks of synthetic daily metrics for SLA, accuracy, refunds, contact rate, partner cancellations, capacity utilization, and delivery timing.
- Customer feedback themes linked to the segments with the highest product and operating drag.
- Requirements and partner review plans for the candidate delivery roadmap.

## Method

1. Aggregate segment-day metrics into weekly operating baselines.
2. Score fulfillment segments using SLA gap, accuracy gap, handoff wait, capacity pressure, customer contact rate, and sentiment.
3. Estimate weekly drag from refunds and customer contacts.
4. Score product opportunities by value, confidence, effort, and partner complexity.
5. Convert the highest-value opportunities into requirements, acceptance criteria, metrics, and partner operations cadence.

## Decision Outputs

- A fulfillment priority queue for product and operations review.
- A business case ranking for delivery product initiatives.
- A PRD-style requirements table with clear acceptance criteria.
- A partner operations plan that defines touchpoints, owners, escalation rules, and review dates.
